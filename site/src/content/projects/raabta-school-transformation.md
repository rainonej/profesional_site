The Raabta School Transformation Program, run by Voyage Educare Foundation, was a sustained effort to improve school quality across 17 partner schools in India — not by importing a curriculum, but by building the capacity of the people inside those schools to transform their own practice.

My role as Curriculum and Program Development Consultant began with a needs assessment: understanding what each school community actually required before designing anything. That diagnostic work shaped everything that followed. I designed a curriculum framework that defined the key areas for training — one that could hold together across 17 different school contexts while remaining responsive to each.

From that framework, I created seven training sessions, each with post-assessments and supporting resources, for an audience of 80 school leaders, 240 teachers, and 750 parents. Designing for parents alongside teachers and leaders was a deliberate choice — school transformation is not possible when parents are treated as recipients of information rather than partners in the work.

This project reinforced something I have come to hold firmly: sustainable school improvement requires building local capacity, not installing external solutions. The curriculum framework I designed was meant to outlast my involvement in it.
