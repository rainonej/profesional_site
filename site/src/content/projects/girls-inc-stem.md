## Overview

This project involves curriculum design and facilitation for Girls Inc. NYC's STEM programming — afterschool and summer experiences for girls in underserved communities across New York City.

## Approach

The curriculum is built around inquiry and identity: activities are designed to connect scientific thinking to students' own questions and experiences, rather than treating STEM as a body of content to be transmitted. Belonging is treated as a design constraint, not an afterthought.

## Key themes

- Access to STEM is necessary but not sufficient — belonging is what keeps girls in
- Hands-on, question-driven activities reduce the gap between "doing science" and "being a scientist"
- Facilitator posture matters as much as curriculum content

## What I've learned

Programs that center girls' questions and lived contexts consistently produce higher engagement than those that prioritize content coverage. The research is clear; the harder work is translating it into day-to-day facilitation.
