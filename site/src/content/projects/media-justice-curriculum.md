As an Arthur Zankel Fellow at Teachers College, Columbia University, I was embedded in PS-187 in Washington Heights to design, teach, and refine a curriculum I called _Media Justice for Social Change_. The fellowship placed me across three 5th grade ICT (Integrated Co-Teaching) classrooms, reaching 100 students whose lives were already saturated with media — but who had rarely been invited to analyze it critically.

The curriculum asked students to move from consumers to critics to creators. We began by examining how news, advertising, and social media construct narratives — whose stories get told, whose get left out, and who benefits from those choices. From there, students developed their own media projects on issues that mattered to their communities: neighborhood displacement, language discrimination, food access, and more.

My role spanned every stage of the work. I spearheaded the curriculum planning using backward design and Universal Design for Learning frameworks, scaffolded activities to meet the range of learners in co-taught classrooms, and liaised between co-fellows, curriculum experts, and project advisors to keep the inquiry grounded and the lessons coherent. I also supported five co-fellows in developing their own lesson plans — curating instructional techniques and learning resources to help each of them build transformative classroom experiences.

The curriculum was awarded the Arthur Zankel Fellowship (2023–2024) and was completed in collaboration with Teachers College faculty and the PS-187 school community.
