Re|Scripted is a nonprofit working at the intersection of history, storytelling, and education. As a Research Assistant, I was brought in to develop an extensive curriculum framework grounded in Universal Design for Learning (UDL) — a framework that insists on building for the full range of learners from the start, rather than retrofitting accommodations after the fact.

The work required holding two things at once: the organization's mission and the academic research that could substantiate and guide it. I conducted literature reviews on relevant topics, synthesizing complex academic sources into structured narrative summaries that could inform curriculum decisions without losing their rigor. I also drafted, proofread, and edited research materials to ensure alignment between the curricular objectives and the research base.

What I value about this project is what it demanded in terms of intellectual translation — taking scholarship that lives in academic journals and making it usable for curriculum designers working on a deadline. That translation is, in many ways, what curriculum development is: moving between what we know and what we can actually build.
